Hephaestus Winery


Exploring associated symbols and imagery

During the brainstorming phase, I conducted some cursory research. I discovered that Hephaestus, the Greek god of metalworking and sculpture, already had a variety of symbols and imagery associated with him. This provided a very fertile starting point for the sketching and thumbnail process. Shapes and iconography such as the anvil and hammer served as a very strong basis for many of the early logo design concepts.

After my exploration, I had several concepts that combined one or more shapes into a single unique mark. I began to focus in on the idea of a logo mark that had strong right angles. This confidently conveys a sense of stability and strength. Both of these qualities tie in well with the concept of metalworking, and give the sense of a bold uniquely crafted wine.

Finding the right modern typeface

A modern wine brand needs to stand out amongst all of the competition on the shelf. A great way to accomplish this is by paring the logo mark with a strong typeface. While exploring many different options, it became obvious that a sans-serif would stand up to the weight of the logo mark without drawing attention away from it. Kautiva Pro is a wonderful sans-serif font that is efficient in both text and display environments. It also serves as a refreshing middle ground between serious geometric and overly humanistic design.

Wine label presentation

For presentation on wine labels, I created a design that feels reminiscent of ancient Greek pottery. The top half of the design prominently displays the logo, while the bottom half is dedicated to the information detailing the specific type of wine.
