The Book of Mothman


Combining inspirations and imagery

During the brainstorming phase, I decided to conduct some cursory research. The Mothman is a popular figure in West Virginian folklore with a wealth of established imagery associated with it. On the other side, The Book of Mormon lent some strong formatting inspiration. I kept coming back to the idea of stacking the font in the same way the title of The Book of Mormon is commonly printed. Both of these avenues of research lead to a strong foundation to start developing initial logo concepts.

After my exploration in the discovery phase, I had several concepts that combined moth shaped imagery, with formatting reminiscent of the cover of The Book of Mormon. From this point, the process shifted towards refining the balance, contrast, and color to help focus in on the final logo design.

Finding the right typeface

For the final design I ended up taking formatting inspiration from The Book of Mormon. This fact ended up greatly influencing the font choice. During the exploration of different fonts, I narrowed my choices down to Serif typefaces. Abril Display ended up satisfying the needs of the design, feeling right at home in a religious context, and displaying well with its bold lettering. The typeface balances well with the logo mark, feeling integrated, while also standing out and remaining legible.

Album cover presentation

For presentation on album covers, things like color and formatting can be changed to fit the needs of the cover art. Artistic freedom is encouraged in this instance. Different fonts can be used if it is deemed necessary for the artwork.
